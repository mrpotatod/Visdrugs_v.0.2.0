VisDrugs 2.0 User Manual

 

VisDrugs 2.0 is a simple and efficient interactive analysis platform for FAERS drug adverse events (Adverse Drug Events, ADEs). VisDrugs 2.0 integrates ASCII-formatted drug adverse event data from the FAERS database for the period 2014Q3-2024Q4, specifically filtering for reports submitted by four types of healthcare professionals: HP (health-professional), MD (Physician), OT (Other health-professional), and PH (Pharmacist). Currently, VisDrugs 2.0 contains over 3,180,000 individual reports from the FAERS database, with each report involving only one drug, thereby avoiding interference from multiple drug adverse reactions. The dataset covers more than 4,000 drug active ingredients and provides over 6,600,000 adverse reaction records to help users accurately analyze drug safety. Through its interactive interface, users simply need to input a drug's active ingredient to analyze and compare potential ADEs that may be caused by the drug.

It is important to note that when reporting data to FAERS, personnel typically use Preferred Terms (PTs) from the Medical Dictionary for Regulatory Activities (MedDRA) to describe medical events. The PTs reported to the FAERS database include not only ADEs PTs caused by the drug's side effects but also some PTs resulting from the patient's disease (i.e., the drug's indications). Therefore, if the analysis targets drug-induced adverse reactions, special attention must be paid to excluding interference from non-ADEs PTs to ensure the accuracy of the analysis results. VisDrugs 2.0 now features an AI real-time analysis function that can help users quickly understand drug-PT relationships and identify potential ADEs to a certain extent.

 

Most Potential ADEs of Target Drugs Module:

Analyzing the most likely adverse drug reactions caused by medications

 

In this module, users can enter the name of the drug active ingredient of interest in the active ingredient field. After typing a few letters, the server will automatically search and display drug active ingredient names in the database that match your input. Users can select the corresponding active ingredient(s) as needed (be sure to enter active ingredients, not drug brand names; multiple selections are allowed). Since some drugs may contain multiple active ingredients or have different formulations, user need to determine which multi-active ingredient drugs or formulations should be included in the analysis based on your research needs.

After selecting the target drug(s), users can name the target drug group in the "Give chosen drugs a name" field (if a name is entered here, it will be used for subsequent PubMed report statistics and AI analysis functions; otherwise, the first content in the active ingredient field will be used as the default name). Then click the "Submit" button, and the server will display the analysis results below. Other optional settings are shown in the figure below.

 

 

The results section includes: Bar plots showing the annual count of PTs by reporting year; Interactive pie chart displaying the top 15 most frequently reported PTs;

 

 

And forest plots. Forest plots are commonly used for the comparative analysis of drug PTs. As illustrated in the detailed results interpretation below, studies typically establish risk signal thresholds based on parameters such as reporting counts of specific PTs, ROR values, and 95% CI lower bounds. A risk signal is confirmed when multiple parameters simultaneously exceed these thresholds. Furthermore, the raw data corresponding to the forest plot is presented in tabular format, which includes hyperlinks to generate AI-interpreted analytical reports.

 

 

Two Drugs Comparison Module:

Compare adverse drug reactions between two drug groups

 

This module enables flexible comparison of adverse drug reactions between two drug groups, and allows subgroup analysis of target medications based on research requirements. Currently, it supports subgroup stratification by age or gender. For example, when performing age subgroup analysis on the top five adverse reactions caused by Moxifloxacin shown in the forest plot above, we observe that Moxifloxacin demonstrates higher ROR values for Dysphoria, Delirium, and Mental disorder in patients over 50 years old compared to those under 50. This suggests that advancing age may increase the risk of these three types of drug-induced adverse reactions.

 

 

 

Multiple Drugs Comparison Module:

Compare adverse drug reactions across multiple drug groups

 

This module enables simultaneous comparison of ADEs across multiple drug groups, facilitating visual identification of shared ADEs (common across compared drugs) and drug-specific ADEs (unique to particular drugs).
